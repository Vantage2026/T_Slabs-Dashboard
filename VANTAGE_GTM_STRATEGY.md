# 1. HOW WE GET THE FIRST USER

## The Pre-Work: Build Your Demo Arsenal

Before outreach, you need ammo. Your synthetic data strategy is smart — here's how to maximize it:

**Create 3 Demo Scenarios:**
1. **"The Dumpster Fire"** — A portfolio with 2 critical blockers, scope creep, a silent team member, and a missed dependency. Show how Vantage catches what humans missed.
2. **"The Hidden Risk"** — Everything looks green, but Vantage surfaces a velocity decline that predicts a 3-week slip. The "aha" moment.
3. **"Cross-Tool Chaos"** — Tasks in Jira depend on Monday cards depend on Asana milestones. Show the unified dependency map no other tool can create.

**Record 90-Second Loom Walkthroughs** of each. These become outreach assets.

## The Outreach Playbook

**LinkedIn DM Template (Cold):**

> Hey [Name] — saw you're leading PM at [Company]. Quick question: how much time does your team spend each week just collecting status updates across tools?
>
> I'm building something that might be relevant — an AI layer that sits on top of Jira/Monday/Asana and generates The Brief (daily digest of what actually matters, risks flagged before they blow up, stakeholder updates auto-drafted).
>
> We're looking for 3-5 design partners to shape the product. Free access, you get a tool purpose-built for your workflow. 90-second demo here: [Loom link]
>
> Worth a look?

**Why this works:**
- Opens with their pain, not your product
- "Design partner" > "beta tester" (status)
- Low commitment ask (watch a 90-second video)
- Specific value prop (The Brief)

**Follow-up sequence:**
- Day 3: "Bumping this — the demo's only 90 seconds if you're curious"
- Day 7: Share a specific insight ("BTW, the risk radar caught a blocker pattern in our test data that saved a team 2 weeks — thought you'd find that interesting")
- Day 14: Final: "Going to assume timing's off — happy to reconnect when you're drowning in status meetings 😅"

**The Close:**

# 2. EARLY ADOPTER PROGRAM DESIGN

## Program Structure: "Founding Partners"

Don't call it beta. Don't call it free trial. Call it **Founding Partners Program**.

Why: Positioning matters. "Beta testers" feel like guinea pigs. "Founding Partners" feel like VIPs shaping something important.

---

## The Offer

| What They Get | Details |
|---------------|---------|
| **Free access** | Full product, no feature limits, entire pilot period |
| **Founding Partner pricing** | 50% off for life when they convert (locked in forever) |
| **Direct line to product** | Slack channel with you, their feedback shapes the roadmap |
| **Priority integrations** | If they need a specific Jira workflow or Monday view, you build it |
| **Logo rights (if they convert)** | "Built with input from [Company]" — social proof for both |

---

## The Ask

| What You Need | Why It Matters |
|---------------|----------------|
| **Connect real accounts** | You need live data to validate the AI actually works |
| **30-min feedback call every 2 weeks** | Structured learning, not just "let us know what you think" |
| **Access to 2-3 end users** | You need to see how ICs use it, not just the buyer |
| **Written testimonial (if satisfied)** | Fuel for future sales |
| **Case study rights (if satisfied)** | "How [Company] reduced status meetings by 60%" |
| **Honest assessment at pilot end** | Would you pay? What would need to change? |

---

## Pilot Timeline

**8 weeks total.** Here's why:

- **Weeks 1-2: Onboarding + Integration**
  - Connect tools, sync data, configure notifications
  - First "Brief" delivered by end of Week 1
  - Daily check-ins to catch integration issues fast

- **Weeks 3-4: Active Usage**
  - They're using it for real workflows
  - First feedback call (Week 3): What's working? What's confusing?
  - You're watching usage data, fixing bugs in real-time

- **Weeks 5-6: Value Validation**
  - Second feedback call (Week 5): Has this changed how you work?
  - Start measuring outcomes (time saved, risks caught, meetings avoided)
  - Ask them to show it to their team

- **Weeks 7-8: Conversion Conversation**
  - Third feedback call (Week 7): The "would you pay?" conversation
  - Present proposed pricing, Founding Partner discount
  - Final call (Week 8): Decision time

**Why 8 weeks, not 2 or 12:**
- Too short: They won't see enough project cycles to validate risk prediction
- Too long: No urgency, you lose momentum, they forget about you
- 8 weeks = 2 full sprints minimum, enough time to catch at least one real risk

| Metric | Target |

**Conversion Target:**
- 3-5 Founding Partners → at least 2 convert to paid

# 3. SOCIAL MEDIA CONTENT STRATEGY

## The Constraint

Tyler isn't making content. So the strategy needs to be:
1. **AI-generatable** (ChatGPT, Claude, Midjourney, Runway, etc.)
2. **Template-driven** (repeatable formats, not one-off creative)
3. **Outsourceable** (clear briefs a $15/hr VA or Fiverr creator can execute)

This rules out: talking-head videos, personal storytelling, "day in my life" content.
This keeps in: text posts, carousels, quote graphics, animated explainers, curated content, UGC repurposing.

---

## Platform-by-Platform Strategy

### LinkedIn — PRIMARY CHANNEL (80% of effort)

**Content Pillars:**

| Pillar | Example Topics | Post Type |
|--------|----------------|-----------|
| **PM Pain Points** (40%) | "The status update trap," "Why your Jira board lies to you," "The hidden cost of tool sprawl" | Text posts, carousels |
| **Risk/Prediction Insights** (30%) | "3 signals a project is about to slip," "Why velocity metrics are misleading," "The blocker no one talks about" | Text posts, data graphics |
| **Product Teasers** (20%) | "What if your PM tool warned you BEFORE the fire?" Demo clips, The Brief previews | Video clips, screen recordings |
| **Social Proof** (10%) | Founding Partner quotes, pilot results, case study snippets | Quote graphics |

**Posting Cadence:** 4-5x/week

**Outsourcing:** $200-400/month for part-time VA + Canva Pro

---

### TikTok — SECONDARY (15% of effort, experimental)

**Content Pillars:**

| Pillar | Example Topics | Post Type |
|--------|----------------|-----------|
| **PM Horror Stories** (40%) | "POV: The project that was 'almost done' for 6 months" | Text-on-screen + trending audio |
| **Tool Roasts** (30%) | "Jira vs Monday vs Asana: the truth no one says" | Quick cuts, meme format |
| **AI Demo Clips** (30%) | "Watch this AI catch a blocker before the PM did" | Screen recording with voiceover |

**Posting Cadence:** 3-5x/week

**Outsourcing:** $300-500/month for Fiverr video editors

---

### Instagram — TERTIARY (4% of effort, crosspost only)

| Content | Format |
|---------|--------|
| LinkedIn carousel reposts | Reformatted for IG dimensions |
| Quote graphics | PM wisdom, stat callouts |
| Reels (TikTok reposts) | Same short-form video |

**Posting Cadence:** 3x/week carousels + 3x/week Reels

---

### Facebook — DEPRIORITIZE (1% of effort)

Organic reach is dead. Auto-crosspost from LinkedIn 1x/week. Only exception: active PM Facebook Groups.
